DIAGRAMATIX — SAMPLE BPMN PROCESS-DESCRIPTION PROMPTS
=====================================================

A graded set, Level 1 (trivial) to Level 10 (the full enterprise works).
Each is written the way a business analyst would describe the process, and
each is phrased to trigger specific BPMN features. They are paste-ready into
the AI Generate -> Plan panel (BPMN). The note after each level says what it
exercises.

HOW THE AI READS YOUR WORDING
-----------------------------
  roles / teams ................... lanes
  an outside party (customer,
    supplier) ..................... black-box pool + message flows
  a named system (Salesforce, SAP,
    Active Directory) ............. system pool
  "either / or" ................... exclusive gateway
  "at the same time / in parallel"  parallel gateway
  "whichever happens first" ....... event-based gateway
  "at any time / if X interrupts" . event subprocess


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 1 — Linear flow
(start -> tasks -> end; no roles)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
A customer places an online order. The warehouse picks the items, packs the
box, and ships it. The process then ends.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 2 — One decision
(exclusive gateway + merge)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
An employee submits a leave request. The manager reviews it and either
approves or rejects it. If approved, HR updates the leave balance and notifies
the employee. If rejected, the employee is notified of the rejection. Either
way the process ends.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 3 — Roles become lanes
(2-3 lanes)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
In the Sales team a rep qualifies a new lead and prepares a quote. The Sales
Manager reviews and approves the quote. Once approved, the rep sends the quote
to the customer and the Finance team raises the invoice.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 4 — External participant
(black-box pool + message flows, send/receive tasks)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
A customer emails a support request. The Support team logs a ticket,
investigates the issue, and emails a resolution back to the customer. The
customer confirms the issue is resolved.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 5 — System pools
(black-box system pools + user tasks)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
When a new employee is hired, HR creates their record in Workday and asks IT
to set up access. IT provisions a laptop and creates the email account in
Active Directory. HR then sends the welcome pack to the new starter.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 6 — Parallel work
(parallel fork/join + lanes)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
After a loan application is received, the bank simultaneously runs a credit
check and verifies the applicant's identity. Once both checks are complete, a
loan officer makes a decision and notifies the applicant.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 7 — Subprocess
(expanded subprocess + exclusive gateway)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
An insurance claim is lodged. It goes through an assessment subprocess that
validates the policy, estimates the damage, and checks for fraud. Based on the
assessment the claim is either approved and paid, or declined with a decline
letter sent to the policyholder.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 8 — Event-based gateway
(the "wait for one of several events" feature)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
After sending a payment request to the customer, the company waits for
whichever of these happens first: the payment is received, the customer
cancels the order, or 48 hours pass with no response. If payment is received,
the order is fulfilled. If the customer cancels, the order is voided. If the
48-hour timeout is reached, a reminder is sent and the order is put on hold.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 9 — Event subprocesses (interrupting + non-interrupting)
(boundary / event handlers)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
A manufacturing run cuts, assembles, finishes, and inspects the product. At
any time during the run, if a defect is reported, work must pause while the
defect is investigated and corrected, then resume. Separately, if the customer
submits a change request mid-production, it should be logged without stopping
the line.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
LEVEL 10 — Full enterprise order-to-cash
(everything: external + system pools, lanes, loop, exclusive + parallel +
event-based gateways, subprocess, event subprocess, data objects)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Order-to-cash for a B2B supplier. The Customer (external) submits a purchase
order by email. In the Sales lane, a rep validates the PO against the price
list in Salesforce and either accepts it or returns it to the customer for
correction, looping until it's accepted. Once accepted, Operations and Finance
work in parallel: Operations reserves stock in the ERP and schedules dispatch,
while Finance runs a credit check. If the credit check fails, the order is
escalated to the Credit Manager, who either grants an exception or cancels the
order. When stock is reserved and credit is cleared, Operations ships the
goods and sends a dispatch notice to the customer, and Finance issues the
invoice to the customer. The company then waits for whichever happens first:
payment received, a dispute raised by the customer, or 30 days elapsing —
payment closes the order, a dispute triggers a resolution subprocess, and the
timeout sends a reminder. At any point before dispatch, if the customer
cancels, a handler releases the stock reservation and closes the order.


-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
USAGE TIPS
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Run these through the 2-phase Plan flow so you can eyeball/edit the
  structure before Apply Layout. Levels 8-10 are the best stress-tests of the
  layout engine and the event-based gateway wiring.
- Levels 4, 5 and 10 are also good Visio round-trip test cases (multiple pools
  + message flows).
